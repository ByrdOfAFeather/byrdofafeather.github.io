Matthew Byrd

Researcher and Software Developer

I graduated from UNC-Chapel Hill in 2021 with a B.S. in Computer Science and a B.S. in Mathematics. During my time as an undergraduate I undertook research in Natural Language Processing under Dr. Shashank Srivastava. I studied how to quantify and predict the difficulty of questions, which resulted in a publication and oral presentation at ACL 2022. Alongside my research, I worked under Dr. Tyson Hedrick to develop software assisting in biological research. One of these projects was a website supporting complex video analysis that was published at the Society for Integrative and Compartive Biology 2021.


In 2022, I started my M.S. in Computer Science at UNC-Chapel Hill. Over the past year I've continued my research with Dr. Srivastava - starting a new project researching methods to classify medical reports. I have also begun freelance web developer work with Caktus Group, including undertaking the role of lead developer on a contract with a team of three people.

I enjoy working on things that have impact, especially projects related to education technology. I hope to employ the skills and knowledge I've developed from both my undergraduate, graduate, and professional experience to help create a more equitable education system.
